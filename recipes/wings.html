Wingstop-style Hot Honey Wings

Description

Deep-fried unbreaded chicken wings or drumsticks coated with hot honey rub. They commonly are served with celery and a ranch dipping sauce, which acts as a cooling agent for the mouth.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds (900g) chicken wings
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup hot sauce (such as Frank's RedHot)
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for extra heat)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the flour, salt, black pepper, and paprika. Coat each chicken wing with the flour mixture, shaking off any excess. Place the coated wings on a wire rack to rest for about 10 minutes.
  2. Fill a large, deep skillet or pot with vegetable oil, ensuring there's enough oil to fully submerge the wings. Heat the oil over medium-high heat until it reaches about 350°F (175°C)
  3. Carefully add the chicken wings to the hot oil in batches, being cautious not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the wings for about 10-12 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove the wings from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  4. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Stir in the hot sauce, honey, apple cider vinegar, garlic powder, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook the sauce for a couple of minutes, stirring occasionally, until well combined and heated through.
  5. In a large bowl, toss the fried chicken wings with the hot honey sauce, ensuring each wing is evenly coated.
  6. Serve the wings immediately and enjoy them while they're still hot and saucy!

These Hot Honey Wings are a delicious and spicy treat inspired by Wingstop. Enjoy!
